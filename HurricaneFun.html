Clearly the cubic interaction effect model is the best approximating model of the set so far with most heterogeneity in residuals accounted for, however there is a problem.Because we effectively are fitting a unique cubic trajectory for each of the hurricanes in the data set we are effectively perfectly predicting a path of each of those specific hurricanes without learning anything about hurricane paths in our sample in general. To put it formally our model effectively over fits the within variation in each hurricane without telling us anything about between variation. This model is thus helpless in predicting new hurricanes for which it doesn’t have pre estimated coefficients, nor can it estimate any fixed non varying characteristics for any of the storms. Random effects and random coefficients come for the rescue in this case, they rely on partial as opposed to full(as in the pooling model where only the global curve is estimated) or no(like with full fixed effects) pooling. Random effects are treated as draws from a normal population of effects with the mean of zero and varience determined by between differences between hurricanes. If the within noise of the particular hurricane is too high or its an outlier it gets penalized based on the square of its noise and pulled towards zero or the global fixed effect during optimization. This is effectively ridge regularization on the interaction model where the coefficients get penalized on the square of their noise whilst the likelihood is maximized, reducing over fitting and preserving between variation that we are interested in.
